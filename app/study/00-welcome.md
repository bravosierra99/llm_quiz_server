# How study guides work

Sometimes a quiz asks about something you were **never actually taught**. That's
not your fault — it just means the material came before the lesson. Study guides
fix that: they're whole-topic explanations you can **read first**, then get quizzed
on once it's stuck.

## The loop

| Step | What happens |
|---|---|
| **1. Hit a gap** | A quiz question covers something you haven't learned. |
| **2. Ask for a guide** | Tap **“📖 Never learned this — request a full guide”** on the feedback screen, or use **Request a topic** at the bottom of the [Study guides](/study) page. |
| **3. Claude writes it** | Claude researches the topic and writes a clear, readable guide — the same way the existing knowledge bases were built. |
| **4. Read it here** | The finished guide shows up on this page. Read it at your own pace. |
| **5. Mark it learned** | Hit **✓ I've learned this** and it moves to *Learned* — out of your way, but never deleted. Changed your mind? *Mark unlearned* brings it back. |

## Good to know

- A guide is **whole-topic** — the background, not a single answer. For help with
  one specific question, use **📚 Teach me this topic** on the quiz screen instead;
  that's a live tutor chat.
- Marking a guide learned is just a **personal hide**. It doesn't delete anything,
  and it doesn't change anyone else's view.
- The fastest way to request a guide is to just **tell Claude the topic** — that's
  what the request box is capturing.

When you've read this, hit **✓ I've learned this** below to clear it.
