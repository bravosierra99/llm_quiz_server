# Wines of the World

This guide is a map. At WSET Level 1 the single most useful skill is reading a
label and knowing *what's in the bottle* — the country, the region, the grape and
the rough style. The tables below give you that in both directions: see a wine and
recall its grape, or start from a country and remember what it makes.

## The one idea that unlocks the rest

**Old World wines (Europe) are named after the *place*. New World wines are named
after the *grape*.**

- A French or Italian label says **Chablis**, **Rioja**, **Chianti**, **Barolo** —
  a *place*. You have to decode the place to know the grape.
- An Australian, American or Chilean label just says **Chardonnay**, **Shiraz**,
  **Malbec** — the grape is printed right there.

So most of the work below is learning to decode Old-World place names. The New
World section is shorter because the label already tells you the grape.

## France

France names wines by place.

| Region | Named wine / appellation | Grape(s) | Colour / style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bordeaux** — **Left Bank** (Médoc, Haut-Médoc; Pauillac, Margaux, St-Estèphe, St-Julien) | Left Bank claret | **Cabernet Sauvignon**-dominant blend (with Merlot, Cab Franc, Petit Verdot) | Red, dry, full-bodied, ageworthy |
| **Bordeaux** — **Right Bank** (St-Émilion, Pomerol) | Right Bank claret | **Merlot**-dominant blend (with Cabernet Franc) | Red, dry, softer/smoother |
| **Bordeaux** (white) | Dry white Bordeaux; Entre-Deux-Mers | **Sauvignon Blanc + Sémillon** | White, dry |
| **Sauternes** (within Bordeaux) | Sauternes; Barsac | **Sémillon + Sauvignon Blanc** (botrytis) | White, **sweet** (noble rot) |
| **Burgundy** — Chablis | **Chablis** | **Chardonnay** (unoaked, steely) | White, dry |
| **Burgundy** — Côte d'Or (white) | White Burgundy: Meursault, Puligny-/Chassagne-Montrachet, Pouilly-Fuissé | **Chardonnay** (often oaked) | White, dry |
| **Burgundy** — Côte d'Or (red) | Red Burgundy: Gevrey-Chambertin, Vosne-Romanée, etc. | **Pinot Noir** | Red, dry |
| **Burgundy** — Mâconnais | Mâcon, Mâcon-Villages, **Pouilly-Fuissé** | **Chardonnay** | White, dry |
| **Beaujolais** (south of Burgundy) | Beaujolais, Beaujolais-Villages, Cru Beaujolais (e.g. Fleurie, Morgon) | **Gamay** | Red, light, fruity |
| **Loire** — Central | **Sancerre**, **Pouilly-Fumé** | **Sauvignon Blanc** | White, dry, crisp |
| **Loire** — Touraine | **Vouvray** | **Chenin Blanc** (dry → sweet, also sparkling) | White |
| **Loire** — Nantais (mouth of the Loire) | **Muscadet** | **Melon de Bourgogne** (NOT Muscat) | White, dry, light |
| **Rhône** — Northern | Côte-Rôtie, Hermitage, Crozes-Hermitage, St-Joseph | **Syrah** | Red, dry |
| **Rhône** — Southern | **Châteauneuf-du-Pape**, **Côtes du Rhône**, Gigondas | **Grenache**-based blends (GSM: Grenache/Syrah/Mourvèdre) | Red, dry |
| **Champagne** | **Champagne** (traditional-method sparkling) | **Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier** | Sparkling (white/rosé) |
| **Alsace** | Varietally labelled (an Old-World exception) | **Riesling, Pinot Gris, Gewürztraminer, Pinot Blanc, Muscat** | White, mostly dry, aromatic |

**Watch out for:**

- **Left Bank = Cabernet Sauvignon; Right Bank = Merlot.** The Gironde estuary
  splits Bordeaux; Médoc (left) is gravel/Cabernet, St-Émilion & Pomerol (right)
  are clay/Merlot.
- **Pouilly-Fumé** (Loire, **Sauvignon Blanc**) vs **Pouilly-Fuissé** (Mâconnais,
  Burgundy, **Chardonnay**) — almost the same name, different wine.
- **Muscadet** is made from **Melon de Bourgogne**, *not* the Muscat grape.
- **Chablis is part of Burgundy** and is 100% Chardonnay, almost always unoaked.
- **Alsace** is the one French region that labels by grape variety.

## Italy

| Region | Named wine | Grape(s) | Colour / style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tuscany** | **Chianti**, Chianti Classico | **Sangiovese** | Red, dry |
| **Tuscany** | **Brunello di Montalcino** | **Sangiovese** (local clone "Brunello") | Red, dry, premium |
| **Piedmont** | **Barolo**, **Barbaresco** | **Nebbiolo** | Red, dry, tannic, ageworthy |
| **Piedmont** | Barbera (e.g. Barbera d'Alba/d'Asti) | **Barbera** | Red, dry, high acid |
| **Piedmont** | **Asti / Moscato d'Asti** | **Muscat (Moscato Bianco)** | Sparkling/semi-sparkling, sweet, low alcohol |
| **Veneto** | **Prosecco** | **Glera** | Sparkling (tank method), off-dry |
| **Veneto** | **Soave** | **Garganega** | White, dry |
| **Veneto** | **Valpolicella**; **Amarone della Valpolicella** | **Corvina**-based blend (Amarone uses dried grapes) | Red — light (Valpolicella) to rich/high-alcohol (Amarone) |
| Northern Italy (Veneto, Friuli, Trentino-Alto Adige) | **Pinot Grigio** | **Pinot Grigio** | White, dry, light |

**Watch out for:**

- **Barolo and Barbaresco are *both* Nebbiolo** (Piedmont).
- **Chianti and Brunello are *both* Sangiovese** (Tuscany).
- **Prosecco = Glera**, made by the **tank (Charmat) method** — unlike Champagne's
  traditional method.
- **Amarone** is a rich, dry red made from semi-dried (*appassimento*) grapes.

## Spain

| Region | Named wine | Grape(s) | Colour / style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rioja** | Rioja | **Tempranillo** (blend) | Red (also white/rosé), oak-aged |
| **Ribera del Duero** | Ribera del Duero | **Tempranillo** (locally "Tinto Fino") | Red, dry |
| **Rías Baixas** (Galicia) | Rías Baixas | **Albariño** | White, dry, aromatic |
| Catalonia (Penedès) | **Cava** | **Macabeo, Parellada, Xarel·lo** (traditional method) | Sparkling |
| **Jerez** (Andalucía) | **Sherry** (Fino, Manzanilla, Amontillado, Oloroso, PX) | **Palomino** (dry styles); **Pedro Ximénez** (sweet) | **Fortified** |

**Watch out for:**

- **Rioja and Ribera del Duero are both Tempranillo** strongholds.
- **Cava** is Spain's traditional-method sparkling (same method as Champagne),
  mostly **Macabeo/Parellada/Xarel·lo**, centred on Penedès.
- **Sherry is a fortified wine from Jerez**; its dry styles are made from
  **Palomino**. **Manzanilla** is sherry from the coastal town of Sanlúcar de
  Barrameda.

## Germany

| Region | Named wine | Grape | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mosel** | Riesling | **Riesling** | White; light, low-alcohol, often off-dry, high acid |
| **Rheingau** | Riesling | **Riesling** | White; often drier, fuller |

**Watch out for:** **Riesling** is Germany's flagship grape, and **Mosel** and
**Rheingau** are the two regions to know — both make Riesling. On a label,
*Trocken* means dry.

## Portugal

| Region | Named wine | Grape(s) | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Douro** | **Port** (Ruby, Tawny, Vintage, LBV, White) | **Touriga Nacional** + native blend | **Fortified**, sweet |
| **Vinho Verde** (Minho, NW Portugal) | **Vinho Verde** | Blend: **Loureiro, Alvarinho (Albariño)**, etc. | White, light, low-alcohol, slight spritz |

**Watch out for:**

- **Port is a fortified wine from the Douro** valley (shipped from the city of Porto).
- **Vinho Verde is a place**, not a grape — a light, fresh, slightly spritzy white
  (the name means "green/young wine").

## The New World

New World wines are usually labelled by **grape variety**, so the label does most of
the work. What's worth memorising is which *region* is famous for which grape.

| Country | Region | Signature wine (grape) | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **USA** | **Napa Valley** (California) | **Cabernet Sauvignon** | Red, full, premium |
| **USA** | **Sonoma** (California) | Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Cabernet | Red & white |
| **USA** | **Oregon** | **Pinot Noir** | Red |
| **Australia** | **Barossa Valley** | **Shiraz** (Syrah) | Red, full, ripe |
| **Australia** | Clare/Eden Valley | Riesling | White, dry |
| **New Zealand** | **Marlborough** | **Sauvignon Blanc** | White, pungent, tropical |
| **New Zealand** | Central Otago | Pinot Noir | Red |
| **Chile** | Central Valley (Maipo, Colchagua) | **Cabernet Sauvignon**, **Carmenère** | Red |
| **Argentina** | **Mendoza** | **Malbec** | Red, full |
| **South Africa** | Stellenbosch / Western Cape | **Chenin Blanc** (a.k.a. "Steen"); **Pinotage** | White (Chenin); Red (Pinotage) |

**Watch out for:**

- The headline pairings: **Napa → Cabernet**, **Oregon → Pinot Noir**,
  **Barossa → Shiraz**, **Marlborough → Sauvignon Blanc**, **Mendoza → Malbec**.
- **Carmenère** is **Chile's** signature red (originally a Bordeaux grape).
- **Pinotage** is **South Africa's** own crossing — **Pinot Noir × Cinsault**.
- **Shiraz** (Australia) and **Syrah** (France) are the **same grape**.
